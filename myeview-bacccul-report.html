Executive Summary

BACCCUL demonstrates a strong digital trust profile relative to peer microfinance and credit union institutions, ranking within the upper tier of assessed organizations nationally and exhibiting positive trust momentum with limited observable threat exposure. The institution's external posture reflects deliberate infrastructure governance, with no elevated-risk entity relationships or suspicious infrastructure associations identified during this assessment period. Several opportunities exist to further strengthen resilience and advance to Shield IV standing, particularly in email authentication enforcement, third-party dependency governance, and certificate lifecycle management — each of which represents a high-confidence, low-friction improvement with meaningful impact on long-term trust score trajectory.

Priority Trust Risk Factors
High Email Authentication Not Fully Enforced ▼
Technical Observation
A DMARC policy is present on the bacccul.cm domain but operates in monitoring mode (p=none) rather than enforcement mode. SPF and DKIM records are present; however, the absence of DMARC enforcement means the policy currently observes without blocking non-compliant mail flows.
Trust Impact Analysis (TIA)
Business Impact

Cybercriminals may find it easier to impersonate BACCCUL through fraudulent emails. This may increase the likelihood of phishing campaigns targeting customers, members, staff, or partners while appearing to originate from the institution.

In a cooperative finance context where member trust is foundational to institutional legitimacy, email impersonation represents a direct reputational and relationship risk — not merely a technical one.

Stakeholders Affected
Customers Employees Business Partners Regulators
Regulatory Relevance
This observation may warrant review against applicable cybersecurity governance expectations and electronic communications security practices under ANTIC Law No. 2010/012 and COBAC operational risk management obligations.
Recommended Action
→ Transition DMARC from monitoring mode (p=none) to quarantine or reject enforcement after validating all legitimate email sources. This is a configuration-level change with no infrastructure disruption and materially strengthens email trust posture.
High Third-Party Script Dependencies — Unverified Integrity ▼
Technical Observation
External JavaScript resources are loaded on publicly accessible pages without Subresource Integrity (SRI) hash verification. These resources originate from two identified third-party domains and are loaded without cryptographic validation on each request.
Trust Impact Analysis (TIA)
Business Impact

If any upstream third-party resource is compromised or modified at source, those changes would be silently delivered to every member or visitor accessing BACCCUL's web presence. The institution would bear reputational and potentially regulatory responsibility for code it did not write and did not validate.

This represents a supply chain trust risk — the kind that does not announce itself until after the event.

Stakeholders Affected
Members Web Visitors IT Operations
Regulatory Relevance
Relevant to third-party digital risk obligations under COBAC R-2025/02 and ANTIC cybersecurity governance expectations for digital service providers.
Recommended Action
→ Add Subresource Integrity (SRI) hash attributes to all externally loaded scripts and stylesheets. Audit third-party dependencies quarterly and remove any that are no longer actively maintained or necessary.
Medium Certificate Renewal Cadence — Monitoring Recommended ▼
Technical Observation
The primary TLS certificate for bacccul.cm has a remaining validity of 47 days at time of assessment. No automated renewal mechanism was observable from external signals. Historical certificate data suggests a manual renewal pattern with a prior lapse of 6 days in the previous cycle.
Trust Impact Analysis (TIA)
Business Impact

A certificate expiry event causes browser security warnings visible to every member, visitor, or partner accessing the institution's digital presence. Even a brief expiry window creates disproportionate trust damage relative to the technical ease of prevention.

Stakeholders Affected
Members Regulators Partners
Recommended Action
→ Implement automated certificate renewal via ACME protocol (Let's Encrypt or equivalent). Set monitoring alerts at 60, 30, and 14 day thresholds. Eliminate manual renewal dependency.
